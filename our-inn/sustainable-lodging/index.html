Sustainable Lodging

We are committed to environmentally responsible building and living. Below is a list of some of the green technologies we are incorporating during construction.

10,000 Gallon Rain Water Cistern

Hidden beneath our terrace will be a 10,000 gallon rain water cistern. It collects the rain water from over half of the roof, which we then use for irrigation and outdoor watering. It's a great way to significantly reduce our water needs, while minimizing storm water runoff from our site.

Geothermal Heating and Air Conditioning

Hidden beneath our gardens and parking will be the heart of the geothermal system. It be able to run not only the heating and air conditioning for all the rooms and public spaces, but also the ice machines, wine cellar, and kitchen refrigerators.

Rain Garden

The rain garden will be located in the back lawn and collect rain water from two downspouts. It is recessed in the ground, uses a special blend of dirt that is 50% sand, and is filled with drought-tolerant plants. When it rains, it fills with storm water and slowly drains, irrigating the plants and filtering the water that seeps into the ground. This is another way to prevent storm water run-off, and control rain water on-site.

Pervious Concrete

Pervious concrete is a recent development that is a concrete (not gravel or asphalt) that allows water to flow through it. Like the rain garden, it also catches and filters rain water.

Native plants

During 2007 and 2008, North Carolina experienced an exceptional drought. To allow our plants to survive, if that should happen again, we have chosen native and drought-tolerant plants for our landscaping. This will also reduce our day-to-day need for water, allowing us to meet our needs with the water captured in our rain water cistern.

Gray Water System

One of our most advanced and extensive environmental features is a gray water system that allows captured drain water to be filtered and recycled within the building. In our case, the laundry water will be filtered and used to flush the toilets - this is why the toilet water is blue. Between our commercial washing machine and our extremely high efficiency toilets, the water from every load of laundry will be enough for 43 toilet flushes. More than a third of the water used in the average American home results from flushing toilets, but we don't anticipate using the city water supply very often for that activity.

Bathroom Fixtures

We use low-flow faucets and shower heads, as well as ultra high efficiency Niagara Flapperless toilets (1.28 gpf). Our hot water boiler is 98% efficient and is variable drive, which means if there is a low demand for hot water, only one burner will heat the water, but if there is a large demand, up to 4 burners will light to provide the needed hot water.

LED Lights

LED lights have an extremely long life (on the order of years), and produce no hazardous materials when discarded. They also use approximately five times less energy than conventional bulbs.

Total Energy Management System

Each guest room has a fully integrated energy management system. The thermostat has an infrared sensor that will detect when the room is occupied. There is also a sensor on the door. If a guest leaves the room, and the infrared sensor doesn't detect any remaining people, after thirty minutes the power will be shut off to most of the room. Of course, there will always be power to the switch for the light, as well as the outlet provided for charging laptops / cell phones, etc. The thermostats will also allow the temperature to fluctuate slightly, which saves a tremendous amount of energy. We will be able to monitor our energy usage, but it is estimated that we can save up to 30% of our energy costs with this system.

Buying Locally

Whenever possible, we buy locally-grown and produced products. We participate in a Farm Share program, and purchase as many ingredients as possible that are grown by local farmers. Our luxurious bath amenities are all natural, vegetable-based, and manufactured in Raleigh.

Recycling

Durham has an active recycling program in which we participate fully.
